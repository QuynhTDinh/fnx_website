# FNX Content & Style Guidelines

When working on this workspace, strictly adhere to the following rules based on the "Anduril Minimalism / Cinematic Global Industrial" style:

## 1. Tone & Voice (Clinical & Technical)
- **Direct & Minimalist:** Present facts and data. Do not use flowery language, sales pitches, or exclamations. Remove all redundant words.
- **Cryptic & Grand:** Focus on scale, capability, and outcome rather than overly detailed "how-tos". The brand should feel like a highly advanced, secure defense/industrial contractor.

## 2. Language Rules (User-Friendly & Professional)
- **Main Language:** Vietnamese. Prioritize clarity and user-friendliness for clients in traditional industries (manufacturing, chemicals).
- **Do NOT translate:**
  - Proper Names: `FNX Group`, `FNX-SC`, `FNX-RD`, `FNX-M`, `BAN XLBM VASI`.
  - Common Universal Terms (only if easily understood): `Hub`, `Module`.
- **Translate to clear Vietnamese:**
  - Avoid cryptic sci-fi English. Use clear, professional Vietnamese equivalents (e.g., instead of "Core Capabilities" use "NĂNG LỰC CỐT LÕI", instead of "Engage" use "HỢP TÁC" or "LIÊN HỆ", instead of "Strategic Affiliate" use "ĐƠN VỊ LIÊN KẾT").
  - Keep the tone professional, minimalist, and direct, but ensure a regular business user immediately understands the value and function.

## 3. Typography & Styling
- **H1, H2, H3:** Always ALL CAPS.
- **Subtitles/Tags:** Always ALL CAPS, small font size (10-12px), wide letter spacing (`tracking-widest`).
- **Paragraphs:** Sentence case. Keep them exceptionally concise.

## 4. UI/UX Rules
- **Color Palette:** Dark Matrix background (`bg-fnx-dark`, `bg-fnx-matrix`), Silver text (`text-fnx-silver`) for readability, Gold (`text-fnx-gold-light`, `border-fnx-gold-dark`) strictly for accents and the Strategic Affiliate (BAN XLBM).
- **Decorations:** Avoid borders on everything. Use `border-white/5` for subtle separations. Use glowing gradients or drop shadows extremely sparingly (only for core nodes).
- **Navigation:** Maintain the SPA feel. Do not introduce traditional dropdown menus or heavy footers. Use cryptic, one-word buttons like "ENGAGE" instead of "Contact Us".
